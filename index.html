Semantic Elements

What are Semantic Elements?

The Importance of Semantic Elements

Semantic Elements are HTML (HyperText Markup Language) elements that communicate meaning to humans concerning the structure of a webpage, rather than just to browsers. By embedding meaning into the structure of a page, semantic elements communicate meaning to other web developers, users utilizing assistive technologies, and search engines. As an example, the <article> tag specfies a self-contained piece of text, such as an individual blog post or newspaper article. Similarly, the <section> tag denotes a grouping of related content.

Web Design Philosophy

Semantic Elements are consistent with the web design philosophy that HTML is for the structure and content of a page whereas CSS (Cascade Styling Sheets) is meant for presentation and style.

Benefits of Using Semantic Tags

  • Make the web more accessible by helping assistive technologies interpret your website
  • Make your webpage more searchable by helping algorithms better understand your content
  • Make your webpage more ingestible for non-native English speakers
  • Make your webpage design and structure easier for other developers to understand
